KEY TERMINOLOGIES USED IN OPTIONS TRADING

Understanding the language of options trading is essential for beginners. Below are some of the key terminologies used in the field:

  1. CALL OPTION: A contract granting the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to buy a specific asset at a predetermined price on or before the expiration date.
  2. PUT OPTION: A contract granting the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to sell a specific asset at a predetermined price on or before the expiration date.
  3. STRIKE PRICE: The price at which the buyer of an option can execute the transaction.
  4. EXPIRATION DATE: The deadline by which the option must be exercised or it becomes worthless.
  5. IN THE MONEY (ITM): Describes an option where exercising leads to a positive cash flow. For calls, when the asset price is above the strike; for puts, when it's below.
  6. OUT OF THE MONEY (OTM): Describes an option that would not lead to a positive cash flow if exercised. For calls, when the asset price is below the strike; for puts, when it's above.
  7. AT THE MONEY (ATM): An option where the asset price is equal to the strike price.
  8. PREMIUM: The cost of the option, paid by the buyer to the seller, influenced by factors like asset price, strike price, and time to expiration.
  9. TIME DECAY: The reduction in an option's value as its expiration date approaches.
  10. IMPLIED VOLATILITY: A measure of expected price volatility of the underlying asset in the future.

These terms form the foundation of options trading knowledge, crucial for navigating and succeeding in the options market.
