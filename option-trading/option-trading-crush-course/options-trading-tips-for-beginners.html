Options Trading Tips for Beginners

  • Always have a trade plan: I’m sure you have heard that before. When trading options, it is critical to control your emotions. Always have a plan to work and always work your plan. No matter what your emotions are telling you to do, don’t deviate from your plan. A basic trading plan should consist of but not be limited to:
    1. How much you are willing to risk per trade?
    2. How will you find opportunities in the market place?
    3. When you will enter the trade?
    4. What is your exit strategy?
      5. Trading with a plan helps you establish more successful patterns of trading and keeps your worries in check.
  • Trade Liquid Options: When you get a quote for any option in the marketplace, you’ll notice a difference between the bid price (how much someone is willing to pay for an option) and the ask price (how much someone is willing to sell an option for). That is known as the spread. Oftentimes, the bid price and the ask price do not reflect what the option is really worth. The real value of the option will actually be somewhere near the middle of the bid and ask. Liquidity in the market means there are active buyers and sellers at all times with heavy competition to fill transactions. The most liquid options will have a penny difference on some contracts. The average daily volume of a contract and its open interest can also be indicators of liquidity. If you are consistently trading illiquid contracts with wider spreads, you are incurring significant slippage. Keep in mind that in the more liquid situations, where there is decent volume and a narrow spread, there is a chance that you can buy an option at the bid or sell at its ask. Over time, that can give you an advantage.
  • Diversify your portfolio: That means diversifying strategies to ensure you have exposure to the unknowns and different market environments. For example, strategies such as straddles allow one to profit from explosive moves in either direction, while bull spreads and other premium selling strategies allow one to profit in calm, directionless environments. Even if option buying is your only strategy, diversity within an options portfolio implies exposure to both calls and puts, different time frames with respect to time played until expiration, and more than one set up for both call and put trades. Don’t put all your eggs in a single basket.
  • Discipline yourself: It is not easy to follow a set of rules consistently unless you are a robot. But habit can be created out of consistent processes. Have the discipline to follow your trading plan for profitable trades. Don’t ignore money management principles. Learn from your trades that result in losses. Don’t be too greedy and don’t be too fearful. Learn to balance your emotions.
