What is the VWAP Trading Strategy?

VWAP stands for Volume Weighted Average Price. These tools are used mostly by short-term traders and in algorithm-based trading programs.

VWAP is often used to measure the trading performance of smart money. Professional traders who work for investment banks or hedge funds need to trade large shares each day and cannot enter or exit the market by buying or selling a large position in stock during the day, and institutional traders compare their prices to VWAP values.

A buy order executed below the VWAP would be considered a good fill for them because the stock was bought at a below-average price (meaning that the trader has bought their large position at a relatively discounted price compared to the market). Opposite for sell

Therefore, VWAP is used by institutional traders to identify good entry and exit points. Conversely, when a professional trader has to get rid of a large position, they try to sell at the VWAP or higher. VWAP has the big advantages of the timeframe you chose, VWAP is the same.

Use of VWAP Trading Strategy

  • VWAP shows who is in control
  • VWAP can determine the market trend at the opening
  • VWAP acts as support and resistance

VWAP shows who is in control

VWAP is an indicator, and it indicates who is in control of the price (the buyers or the sellers). When a stock is traded above the VWAP, the buyers control the price, and there is a buying demand. When a stock price breaks and closes below the VWAP, it is safe to assume that the sellers are gaining control over the price.

  1. If VWAP is rising, then it shows buyers in control
  2. If VWAP is falling, it shows sellers in control
  3. If VWAP is flat, it indicates no one controls the market, and the price is in a trading range.

Used as Support and Resistance

Smart Money Buy below VWAP and sell above the VWAP; if a large order comes to market, then they buy from VWAP, so when the price is unable to close below VWAP and gets rejected from VWAP and creates a shadow or engulfing or outside bar, this confirms the support and resistance.

Price action and volume confirm the support and resistance

  

VWAP Trading Strategy USED TO DETERMINE THE MARKET TREND

Observations

  1. For bullish trend days, the market stays above the VWAP.
  2. For bearish trend days, the market stays below the VWAP.
  3. For ranging sessions, the market stays around the VWAP, which remains more or less flat.

These observations show that the VWAP has great potential for helping traders identify the market trend.

Note: To avoid trading at the opening of a trading session:

After the market opens, the price bars overlap with the VWAP. In the first five minutes, unknown heavy trading occurs between the overnight shareholders, new investors, and institutional traders. According to our method, you cannot judge the market bias until the market tries to move away from it.

After volatility decreases ten to fifteen minutes into the Open, the stock will move toward or away from the VWAP. This test is to see if a large investment bank is waiting to buy or sell. If a large institutional trader(smart money) aims to buy a significant position, the stock will pop over the VWAP and move even higher. This is a good opportunity for us to go long. Opposite for short selling. If there is no interest in the stock from market makers institutions or smart money, the price may trade sideways near VWAP. The best option would be to stay away from that stock.

If they push away from the VWAP and have good follow-through, assume a trending session. You can then consider momentum trades in the direction of the trend. Assuming a sideways session if the market rejects the push away back to the VWAP. Consider taking mean-reversion trades in this case.

The characteristics of sideways markets are

  • Price often near VWAP, Point of Control, or other equilibrium prices
  • Price stays the whole day in the opening range (the span of the first hourly candle)
  • Inside- and outside candles near each other
  • Many crossings of VWAP

LIMITATION OF VWAP Trading Strategy

  1. VWAP Trading Strategy Lags tend to increase as the day passes
  2. Cannot be used at the opening of the day
  3. Require supporting price action for entry

VWAP Trading Strategy PRINCIPLES

Two words are used here( PVWAP and VWAP). PVWAP is the end of the VWAP value of the previous day. VWAP is the current day VWAP. PVWAP can be obtained by plotting a straight horizontal line on the chart and looking at where it was plotted at 3:30 PM. VWAP is obviously current-day VWAP, which can be obtained by plotting the VWAP indicator.

CONDITION

  • Do not play stock long that is below the VWAP
  • Do not play stock short if above the VWAP
  • If the way is extended from VWAP, then a play reversal is okay, but the target has to be the VWAP.
  • If the price trades above PVWAP, it is bullish, and we look for a buy entry.
  • If the price trades below PVWAP, it is bearish, and we look for a sell entry.

CONFLUENCE: USE VWAP indicator with price action and volume

ENTRY

  1. Buy Entry – For any 5-minute candle completely above both VWAP and PVWAP, the buy entry will be above the height of that candle.
  2. Sell Entry – For any 5-minute candle that is completely below both PVWAP and VWAP, the sell entry will be below the low of that candle.

VWAP False Breakout (TRAP)

Strong Stock will stay and trade above VWAP if there is buying pressure from institutional traders. If a large investment bank is interested in taking the position, a stock will often stay above VWAP and keep moving above VWAP. But if no large institutions are behind the stock, or if they fill all their orders, the stock will move back to VWAP and often “lose it,” meaning it will drop and trade below the VWAP. This is a sign for short sellers to start shorting it. On the other hand, when a stock below the VWAP bounces back and breaks out above the VWAP, it means the buyers are gaining control, and short-sellers desperately have to cover. Smart day traders chase the fleeing shorts by going long to ride the momentum and “squeeze the shorts.” Price action and volume play a major role.

VWAP Trading Strategy Summary:

The Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP) is a trading benchmark often used by institutional traders. It is calculated by taking the dollar amount traded for a particular security and dividing it by the total shares traded. It gives traders insight into the trend and value of a security. A VWAP strategy is designed to identify the liquidity points and the average security price weighted by volume, which can be used for determining entry and exit points.

Understanding VWAP Trading Strategy:

  • Meaning: VWAP is the average price a security has traded throughout the day, based on volume and price. It is important because it gives traders insight into a security’s trend and value.
  • Calculation: It is calculated by adding up the dollars traded for every transaction (price multiplied by the number of shares traded) and dividing by the total shares traded for the day.

VWAP Trading Strategy:

Here’s how a trader might use VWAP in their strategy:

1. Trend Identification:

  • Above VWAP: If the price is above the VWAP line, it may indicate that the stock is bullish.
  • Below VWAP: If the price is below the VWAP line, it may suggest bearish sentiment.

2. Trade Execution:

  • Buying: Traders may consider buying when the price is near or just above the VWAP line in an uptrend, which can signify the price is favorable.
  • Selling: In a downtrend, selling or shorting when the price is near or just below the VWAP line may be considered as it could indicate the price is relatively high.

3. Confirmation with Other Indicators:

  • Volume: Consider increasing volume as a confirmation when the price crosses the VWAP line to validate the move.
  • Price Action: Candlestick patterns and price action can provide additional entry or exit signals.

4. Pullbacks and Breakouts:

  • Pullbacks: After a breakout, prices often pull back to the VWAP line, which can act as a support or resistance level.
  • Breakouts: A price breakout from the VWAP line can signal a strong move. Traders often wait for a confirmed breakout with high volume before entering a trade.

VWAP as Support and Resistance:

  • Dynamic Support/Resistance: VWAP can act as a dynamic support and resistance level throughout the trading day.
  • Pivot Point: Prices may pivot around the VWAP line, presenting potential trading opportunities on pullbacks and breakouts.

Trading Rules with VWAP:

  1. Risk Management: Always use stop losses, as prices can deviate significantly from the VWAP line.
  2. Volume: VWAP is volume-weighted, so high volume levels are essential for confirming its signals.
  3. Time of Day: VWAP is more meaningful as the day progresses. Be cautious using it right after the market opens due to volatility.
  4. Avoid Chasing: Do not chase the price if it has moved far from the VWAP line, as a reversal could occur.

Limitations of VWAP Trading Strategy:

  • Intraday Only: VWAP resets at the beginning of each trading day, so it only applies to intraday trades.
  • Large Orders: Institutional traders may use VWAP to place large orders, which could influence the VWAP line.
  • Lagging Indicator: Since VWAP is an average, it is inherently a lagging indicator.

Example of a VWAP Trading Strategy:

Imagine the price of a stock starts the day below the VWAP line but then crosses above it with increasing volume. This might indicate bullish momentum. A trader could take a long position after this crossover if confirmed by additional indicators or price action. The trader might place a stop-loss order just below the VWAP line and set a profit target at a previous high or another resistance level.

 

When trading with VWAP, as with any strategy, it is crucial to ensure that it fits within your overall trading plan and risk management rules. No single indicator should be used in isolation, and VWAP is most effective when combined with other technical analysis tools and methodologies.
