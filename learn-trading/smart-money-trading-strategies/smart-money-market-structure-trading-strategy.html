Principles of Smart Money Market Structure in Order Block Trading

Price moves within a structural of support and resistance. A breakout of the structural of support or resistance will lead to price movement in the next area of the support or resistance.

Strong Low (SL)

The market structure was high when the price broke, and the low point became a strong low. A Strong Low is The Low that causes Manipulation and Break Structure (resistance).

Weak High or Low

Fresh high in an uptrend and fresh low in a downtrend. Weak Low/High is the Low that fails To Break Structure (WEAK HIGH OR LOW PRODUCED ALWAYS FROM A strong High or Low).

 
  • For every strong LOW, there is a weak High
  • For every strong High, there is a weak Low

When Does Supply/Demand Break?

Supply and Demand levels eventually break after a zone is tested many times or during a strong move. Due to the remaining orders being triggered and gradually removed or an overwhelming number of orders in the opposite direction breaking the level.

Smart Money Market Structure Trading Strategy Summary:

The market structure, in this context, refers to the way markets move and form trends and ranges. Smart money traders analyze these structures to determine where large institutional investors will likely enter or exit positions. 

 

Key Concepts in Smart Money Market Structure Trading Strategy

  • Accumulation and Distribution: Smart money often accumulates positions slowly and discreetly during quiet market periods and distributes or sells these positions during periods of high liquidity or market hype.
  • Stop Hunts and False Breakouts: Smart money is known to push the market to certain levels to trigger stop-loss orders or to create false breakouts to accumulate or distribute positions at favorable prices.
  • Volume Analysis: Analyzing trading volumes can provide clues about the presence of smart money. High volume at certain price levels may indicate smart money activity.
  • Price Levels and Support/Resistance: Identifying key price levels, such as historical support and resistance, where smart money might enter or exit the market.
  • Market Sentiment: Understanding the prevailing market sentiment can help identify points where smart money might go against the crowd.

Trading Strategy

  • Identify Accumulation/Distribution Phases: Look for periods when the price moves sideways or in a range, which might indicate accumulation or distribution by smart money.
  • Watch for Breakouts with High Volume: A breakout from a range with significantly increased volume can signify smart money initiating a new trend.
  • Track False Breakouts and Stop Hunts: Be cautious of false breakouts and movements that seem to target common stop-loss levels.
  • Use Technical Analysis Tools: Utilize tools like volume indicators, price action analysis, and moving averages to identify potential smart money movements.
  • Contrarian Approach: Consider taking positions against the prevailing market sentiment when other indicators suggest smart money might be doing the same.
  • Risk Management: Always employ strict risk management rules. Remember, aligning with smart money doesn’t guarantee success, as markets can be unpredictable.

The Smart Money Market Structure Trading Strategy involves analyzing market behavior to align with institutional investors’ actions. It requires a good understanding of market dynamics, volume analysis, and price action. While this approach can be rewarding, it is also complex. It comes with risks, making it essential to approach with caution and a solid understanding of market fundamentals and risk management.
