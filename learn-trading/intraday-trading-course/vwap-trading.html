WHAT IS VWAP Trading?

Volume weighted average price (VWAP). These tools are used mostly by short-term traders and in algorithm-based trading programs. VWAP is often used to measure the trading performance of smart money. Professional traders who work for investment banks or hedge funds must trade large shares each day and cannot enter or exit the market by buying or selling a large position in stock during the day. Institutional traders compare their prices to VWAP values.

Two words are used here (PVWAP and VWAP)

PVWAP is the end of the VWAP value of the previous day. VWAP is a current-day VWAP. PVWAP can be obtained by plotting a straight horizontal line on the chart and looking at where it was plotted at 3:30 PM. VWAP is obviously current-day VWAP, which can be obtained by plotting the VWAP indicator.

WHAT IS VWAP PULLBACK STRATEGY

FIRST UNDERSTAND WHAT IS PULLBACK

A pullback is a price movement that moves in against the trend. It is a temporary price movement before it resumes back into the main market direction. Pullbacks are sometimes referred to as price Corrections or retracement.

VWAP PRICE PULLBACK ENTRY

CHARACTERISTICS of WEAK PULLBACK

  1. Correction(depth of pullback) must be small and without strong momentum candlestick.
  2. Volume decreases / low volume correction
  3. A great mix of red and green candles with light volume
  4. Closes towards the middle with wicks

LOGIC OF VWAP PRICE PULLBACK ENTRY

If a stock moves strongly in the morning, supported by smart money, it respects VWAP. When a stock is traded above the VWAP. If VWAP is rising, then it shows buyers in control. When a stock is traded below the VWAP. If VWAP is falling, it shows sellers in control.

 

Step to follow

  1. Find the stock in a clear trend up (HH/HL) or trend down(LH/LL). Look for at least 2/3 candles in the same direction with a high volume.
  2. THEN wait for the price to pullback(WEAK) towards VWAP
  3. Check whether VWAP rejected the price or not. (look for rejection from VWAP)
  4. If rejected go with the initial move
  5. Don’t buy aggressively until this stock heads in the initial direction.
  6. Check price action around opening range high or opening range low
  7. Those stocks that trade back above the opening range price are likely to go even higher. This is because of a new bull entry plus a short cover buy order, so after the reaction period, the market set the tone of the morning trend

 

TIME PULLBACK

TIME CORRECTION(Stock to digest the directional move is through a time correction. In a time correction, the stock moves horizontally, with volatility trendless. Generally, a strong trend has time correction. Because the pullback is shallow, it isn’t easy to time your entry on a pullback. Instead, you can look to trade the breakout.

What is happening here?

  1. The initial upward movement shows the direction of major interest. Then, a stock meets resistance and consolidates under this level. If the stock is strong enough to stay close to the resistance level without sharp retracement, it means that the path of least resistance is still upward and that the stock is likely to continue in the same direction as soon as it digests the distribution.
  2. Smart money slowly and discreetly accumulates its positions in sideways price action. There, they can hide their activity perfectly. A sideways price action is a place where big institutions are getting ready for action
  3. Once bulls are confident that the bears will fail to reverse the trend, bulls buy again with a tighter stop loss.
  4. We prefer the range of the consolidation to be narrow

Step to follow

  1. Find the stock in a clear initial move with high volume.
  2. For bullish breakouts, look for a price to hug the top of the range. For upward breakouts, trade only those situations where the price most often closes above the middle of the opening range. Downward breakouts from the opening range do best when the price most often resides below the range’s midpoint.
  3. The price should be above both PVWAP and VWAP.
  4. Trade with the trend. In a bear market, downward breakouts tend to make more money than upward breakouts in intraday trading. In bull markets, upward breakouts make more money.
  5. Look, Break out with volume, and clean the candle.
  6. After the breakout, the stock exhibited bullish price action. I mean, breakout should follow through.

What invalidates our setup?

  1. Price takes too much time during consolidation.
  2. Price should not break the initial move high(for long entry)during consolidation for an upside breakout.
  3. The first candle has both upper and lower long wick

 

VWAP Trading Summary:

VWAP, or Volume Weighted Average Price, is a trading benchmark that investors and traders often use to determine the average price a security has traded at throughout the day, based on both volume and price. It measures the average price at which a stock is traded over the trading horizon (usually one day). VWAP is important because it provides traders with insight into a security’s trend and value.

Here are some key points about VWAP trading:

Definition: VWAP is calculated by adding up the dollars traded for every transaction (price multiplied by the number of shares traded) and dividing by the total shares traded for the day.

Purpose: The purpose of VWAP is to provide a running total throughout the day that ends at the average price for the day when the market closes.

Usage: Traders use VWAP to ensure they get a reasonable order price. For example, if a trader buys below the VWAP, they get a better-than-average price for the day.

Day Trading: It’s most commonly used for day trading. If the price is above the VWAP line, it’s considered a good time to buy, and if the price is below the VWAP line, it may be a good time to sell.

 

Trend Indicator: Some traders use VWAP as a trend indicator. If the price is above VWAP, they may consider the trend to be up; conversely, if the price is below VWAP, they may consider the trend to be down.

Benchmarking: Institutional traders also use VWAP as a benchmark to compare the relative execution price of trades. For instance, buying lower than VWAP is often considered a good trade.

Limitations: VWAP is a cumulative indicator, which means it can only be used for intraday trades. It resets at the beginning of each trading day.

 

VWAP Trading Strategies:

  • Mean Reversion: Traders might assume that the price will revert back to the VWAP at some point. Therefore, if prices are extended far above or below the VWAP, they may anticipate a reversal.
  • Momentum Trades: If the price breaks through the VWAP in a strong trend, traders may take this as a sign of strong buying or selling momentum and trade in the breakout direction.
  • Price Crosses: When the price crosses the VWAP line, traders may take it as a signal to enter a position. A cross above might signal a long position, while a cross below might signal a short position.

Execution: Some institutional traders will use VWAP to plan large orders so that their trades minimize market impact and achieve a lower average price than the VWAP over the day.

Risk Management: Like any trading strategy, combining VWAP with other analyses and indicators is important. Traders should also employ risk management techniques, such as setting stop losses.

VWAP can be valuable in the trader’s toolkit, particularly for short-term, intraday trades. It’s used not just as an indicator for trade entry and exit but also as a performance evaluation tool to understand how efficient a trader’s execution is relative to the market.
